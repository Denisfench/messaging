SOC-NET Fusion / Vetting Platform

The Need

  • SOC-NET members have already requested the capacity to vet / confirm early threat reports or developing incident information (bombing, shooting, other disruptive events) where members of the community can share a report and its source for others to validate / augment / dispute optimally from alternate sources.
  • SOC-NET members have also indicated that they would like to increase the sharing of wider global events having primarily focused most recently on the metro-New York area.
  • If vetting is sought for multiple threats and events across the globe on a regular basis, the current email distribution approach for SOC-NET members may result in substantial volumes of information in personal inboxes.

Potential Response

  • A real time repository / platform versus simple e-mail traffic as a means of vetting uncertain information.
  • Primary users would be on-duty analysts versus administrative contacts but all would have access
  • SOC-NET members could send incident-related information to via e-mail / text or enter directly on the platform.
  • Could be an online dashboard that analysts could keep opened at all times or only during events.
  • Could be organized automatically along various categories or threads based on geography / location or type of event.
  • Members won’t get barraged with e-mails in their personal inboxes.
  • There could be one email at the start of an open incident with a link to the platform
    • Users could sign up for alerts for particular countries / cities only
  • Reports could utilize a standard format / tags that an algorithm can easily recognize
    • SUBJECT: “[Incident Type] - [City], [Country],” ex. “Vehicle Ramming – Paris, France”
  • Vetting / Validating Aspect: Could have buttons along the lines of “I’ve seen this too” “I can confirm this” or “I’ve seen conflicting information” that members can click, show a percentage of each or color-code the item based on if it’s mostly agreed-upon or not
